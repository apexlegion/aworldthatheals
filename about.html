Healing should never be limited by money or access

A World That Heals is a free, public library of trustworthy mental health, wellbeing, and recovery resources. Our belief is simple: no one, anywhere, should be deprived of help simply because they can't afford it or don't know where to look.

This is not therapy, and it is not an app that claims to fix you. It's a calm place to understand what you're going through and to find the world's best 100% free resources — articles, tools, apps, communities, and crisis support — gathered in one place.

What we promise

  • Only genuinely free resources — no paywalls dressed up as help.
  • No ads, no sponsored listings, no affiliate links, no upselling.
  • No accounts and no tracking. We don't want your data.
  • Evidence-informed, and honest about what's proven versus emerging or traditional.
  • Accessible, mobile-first, and built to work even offline.
  • Global-first, with crisis support you can find by country.

How to read the labels

Every topic and many resources carry a label so you can weigh them fairly:

  • Evidence-based — well-supported by scientific research.
  • Emerging evidence — promising and reasonable, with research still developing.
  • Traditional / spiritual — rooted in long-standing practice or wisdom rather than clinical trials.

An important boundary

This platform is for education and navigation. It does not diagnose conditions and cannot replace professional care from a doctor or therapist. If you may be in crisis or in danger, please use our emergency support page to reach a real person now.

What's inside

The library now spans: an encyclopedia of feelings and topics; mindfulness & breathwork practices; psychological approaches like CBT, ACT and DBT; a directory of teachers and authors; addiction recovery fellowships; genuinely free apps; stories of healing; communities; and a live meeting finder that shows times in your own timezone.

It will keep growing — with fuller content translation, more topics and practices, and automated freshness checks — and it will always stay free.
